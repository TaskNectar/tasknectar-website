Keep Quality High & Manage File Size

Two factors determine the size and quality of your final PDF: image resolution and embedded fonts. Here‚Äôs how to keep things crisp without creating a monster file:

  • Use reasonable scan settings: 150‚Äì300 DPI is enough for most office documents; higher DPI inflates file size with little benefit for text.
  • Avoid repeated re-saving: Each export or print-to-PDF pass can bloat or rasterize pages.
  • Compress images before merging: If any PDFs are mostly photos, pre-compress them.
  • Check fonts: Embedded fonts keep layout consistent across devices.
